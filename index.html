Pricing & Deal Structure Forecast

We used 2025 through February 2026 deal and customer data to estimate how key pricing variables — and their interaction effects — influence win rate, churn, and LTV:CAC. The purpose is to compare future pricing scenarios and identify deal structures that are more likely to produce strong conversion, retention, and unit economics.

Executive takeaways
1

Deal structure matters

MRR, onboarding, waived months, and discounts all influence outcomes, but the effect depends on the full package rather than any single lever.

2

Interactions are important

Onboarding can behave differently when paired with waived months or higher MRR, so the calculator evaluates combinations instead of isolated inputs.

3

Optimize net value

The best structure is not always the one with the highest win rate. The goal is to balance conversion, churn risk, and LTV:CAC.

Important limitations: These outputs are based on machine learning models trained on observed 2025–February 2026 data. We observe final reported deal terms in HubSpot/source systems, not the full negotiation path, original anchor price, rep-level context, competitive dynamics, or every concession offered during the sales process. Use the results to compare likely scenario performance, not as precise causal rules for individual deals.
