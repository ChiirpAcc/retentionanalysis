CHIIRP Unit Economics
Corrected tenure · contract-date cohorts · net revenue · win rate adjusted
Executive summary
358 contract accounts (2025Q2–2026Q1). 1,446 Stripe accounts total. Net revenue = gross MRR − discounts − coupons − refunds. Tenure corrected for active billing lag. Monthly churn rate: 2.8% (from 2025Q3, 7 months observed). CAC = $7,862 (Q1 2026 avg).
Revised findings for leadership
1. Monthly churn is 2.8%, not 5%. The corrected data (fixing billing lag artifacts) shows much better retention than the prior analysis. 71% of customers survive to month 12 at this rate. This fundamentally changes the unit economics.

2. Free months boost win rate — removing them cut deal flow nearly in half. Q3 2025 (62% had free months): 10.2% win rate → 134 deals. Q1 2026 (3% had free months): 5.3% win rate → 68 deals. That's 66 fewer deals/quarter, representing ~$554K in lost cohort LTV. Free months aren't just a retention question — they're a top-of-funnel acquisition lever.

3. At $1,500/mo, a 3-free-month deal still generates +$4,221 net of CAC. Breakeven is month 9 (vs month 5 without free months). If offering 3 months free is what maintains win rate, the math overwhelmingly supports it.

4. Accounts under $700/mo are underwater at current CAC. $700/mo × 12 months × 71% survival = $7,213 gross LTV. At $7,862 CAC that's −$649. These accounts need either higher pricing, lower-cost channels, or onboarding fees to break even.

5. 2026Q1 is structurally the strongest cohort. $1,008 avg gross MRR, 100% active, 0% free months. Projected 12-month LTV of $10,305. But the 5.3% win rate means we're closing half as many deals as Q3 2025.

Win rate & free months tradeoff
The most important finding in this analysis: free months significantly impact deal flow. Removing them correlated with a 48% decline in win rate.
The real cost of eliminating free months
The per-account cost of 3 free months at $1,500/mo is ~$3,000 in delayed revenue (breakeven moves from month 5 → 9).

But the win rate data tells a bigger story: Q3 2025 closed 134 deals at 10.2% WR (62% had free months). Q1 2026 closed 68 deals at 5.3% WR (3% had free months). That's 66 fewer deals per quarter.

At $1K avg MRR × $10.3K projected 12-month LTV = $680K in lost cohort LTV per quarter. The aggregate cost of 3-month concessions on 62% of those 134 deals would have been ~$250K. The tradeoff is not close.

Caveat: Win rate also declined due to pricing increases, seasonal effects, and CAC changes. Free months are not the only variable. But they are the largest observable change between Q3 2025 and Q1 2026.

MRR vs win rate vs free months
The tradeoff: higher pricing + fewer free months = fewer deals
2025Q3 is the sweet spot in hindsight: $769 avg MRR, 10.2% WR, 134 deals. Q4 added $18/mo in MRR but lost 48 deals. Q1 added $239/mo but lost another 18. The MRR gain per account doesn't compensate for the volume loss at the portfolio level.
CAC trend and LTV/CAC ratio
Rising CAC is compressing margins
LTV/CAC improved from Q3→Q1 (1.40x→1.27x) despite rising CAC, because MRR gains are outpacing CAC increases. But the target of 3x LTV/CAC at 36 months requires a 2.8% monthly churn or better — achievable based on 2025Q3 data.
