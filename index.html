Executive summary
358 contract accounts from 2025Q2–2026Q1. Revenue is net of discounts, coupons, and refunds. Churn rate from 2025Q3 (76 accounts, 6 months observed). CAC matched to each cohort's acquisition quarter.
Five findings for leadership
1. Monthly churn is 2.8%. From our most mature clean cohort (2025Q3, 6 months). 71% of customers survive to month 12. This is derived from actual Stripe billing data with tenure corrected for active accounts not yet invoiced for the current month.

2. Removing free months correlated with a 48% drop in win rate. Q3 2025 (62% had free months): 10.2% win rate, 134 deals. Q1 2026 (3% had free months): 5.3% win rate, 68 deals. That's 66 fewer deals per quarter. At $10.3K projected LTV per deal, the lost cohort LTV is ~$680K/quarter — far exceeding the per-account cost of 3 free months ($2,900–$4,400 depending on MRR).

3. Accounts under $700/mo are underwater at current CAC. $700/mo with no onboarding and no free months generates $7,212 in 12-month LTV against $7,862 in 2026Q1 CAC. Breakeven is month 14 — beyond the safe observation window. The pricing floor needs to be $800+/mo for sustainable unit economics.

4. 2026Q1 is the highest-MRR cohort. $1,008 avg gross MRR (up from $689 in Q2). But the 5.3% win rate means we're acquiring half as many accounts. The MRR gain per account does not compensate for the volume loss at the portfolio level.

5. CAC rose 66% from Q2 to Q4 2025. $4,988 → $8,267. This compresses margins from both sides — higher cost to acquire, fewer deals closing. Q1 2026 improved slightly to $7,862 but remains elevated vs. the first half of 2025.

The portfolio-level cost of eliminating free months
Q3 2025 closed 134 deals at 10.2% win rate (62% had free months). Q1 2026 closed 68 deals at 5.3% (3% had free months). That's 66 fewer deals per quarter.

At $10,303 projected 12-month LTV (at $1K MRR), the lost cohort LTV is ~$680K per quarter. The cost of offering 3 free months on 62% of 134 deals (at avg $769 MRR) would have been ~$252K in delayed revenue. The net loss from eliminating free months: ~$428K per quarter in unrealized LTV.

Caveat: Pricing increases, seasonal variation, and rising CAC all contributed. Free months are the largest single observable change, but not the only one.
