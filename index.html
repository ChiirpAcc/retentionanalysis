OB adoption fell from 97% in 2024Q4 to 34% in 2026Q1. Average fee fell from $755 → $225. Based on cohort analysis, this is likely to cost $1,400–$3,000 in LTV per mid/high customer when the cohort matures.

Cohort-controlled analysis. Clean data (2024Q3+) only. Quarter fixed effects applied in regression. Effect measured as change in tenure rate vs $0 baseline within same cohort quarter.
Final verdict — onboarding fees
There is no case for waiving onboarding on accounts over $400/mo. The $750+ tier adds $1,400–$3,000 in LTV vs $0 onboarding for mid and high-priced customers. For premium ($1K+), it adds $2,980. The only exception is low-priced accounts (<$400/mo) where onboarding fees reduce LTV. The partial ($500–$749) tier is consistently the worst — charge full or waive it, never discount it.

Aggregate signal is weak — segment signal is strong
The overall regression says −$44 per $100. That's because low-priced accounts (where OB hurts) average out against premium accounts (where it helps). Look at the tier-level data, not the average.
Never charge partial onboarding to premium accounts
$500–$749 onboarding for $1K+/mo customers costs −$6,552 in LTV vs $0 onboarding. If an AE is discounting onboarding on a premium deal, that's the worst possible outcome. Full or waive.
Charge full onboarding on the new $1,500+/mo deals
At the new pricing tier, full onboarding adds meaningful upfront revenue and, based on the data, does not harm retention for premium customers. The current 34% adoption rate in 2026Q1 is leaving money on the table.
Free months effect
Cohort-controlled analysis. The naive comparison showed −$100 LTV per free month — that was almost entirely time bias. After cohort controls the aggregate effect is near zero. But the structure of the free months offer matters a lot.
Final verdict — free months
Free months are not a retention killer — but 1–2 months is. The 3-month structure adds $1,100–$1,650 in LTV vs no discount across mid-to-premium tiers. 1–2 months destroys $2,400–$12,900 depending on price tier. The rule is simple: if you give anything, make it 3 months. Never 1–2. A deal with 3 free months is better than no deal in almost all cases.

CAC breakeven analysis
CAC = $7,000 (Q1 2026). New standard pricing = $1,500/mo. Survival curve based on actual 2025Q1 cohort data (120 customers, 15 months of observation). This is the best available proxy for how new accounts will behave.
Key finding
At $1,500/mo, every scenario makes CAC breakeven well within expected tenure. The question isn't "will we break even" — it's "how much does each policy decision cost us in total ROI." No-free-month, full-onboarding deals break even at month 4.5 and generate +$12,212 net ROI by month 15. Three free months pushes breakeven to month 7.5 and reduces 15-month ROI by ~$4,400. That's the real cost of 3 free months at $1,500/mo.

Policy scenario analysis
What happens to total portfolio LTV if we change onboarding or free month policy universally? Modeled on 2024Q3+ cohorts using observed tenure rates by price tier.
Bottom line
A blanket "everyone pays $750+ onboarding, no one gets free months" policy is essentially revenue-neutral at the portfolio level — you get +$200/customer upfront but lose ~$297 in MRR-based LTV. Total LTV changes by only −$97. But this masks a split: it helps low-tier customers and hurts premium ones. Forcing onboarding on premium customers who currently don't have it disrupts what's already working.

Universal policy doesn't move the needle
All three scenarios produce total LTV within $100 of current. The effects cancel each other out across the portfolio. Blanket policy is the wrong tool.
Segmented policy is where the value is
Charge $750+ OB on mid/high/premium. Waive on low. Allow 3-mo free on mid/high/premium if needed to close. Ban 1–2 month and partial OB deals. This captures the LTV upside without the drag.
Premium customers are most sensitive
Forcing universal OB on premium accounts (who currently average $0 OB but have 88.9% tenure rate) costs −$605 in LTV. They're your best customers. Don't disrupt what's working.
