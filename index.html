Recommendation
The safest standard structure is 1 free month + onboarding equal to roughly one month of MRR. For a current $2,250 MRR customer, that means $2,250 onboarding and one free month. Do not treat CAC as a model input; use CAC as the horizontal threshold the projected cumulative cash curve has to cross.
Decision rule: if Sales needs a concession, give the 1 free month before materially cutting onboarding. Cutting onboarding immediately delays CAC recovery. One free month delays MRR by one month, but preserves the upfront cash that makes payback possible.
What this calculator does
The calculator estimates a 12-month churn probability from the controlled churn model, converts it into a monthly churn hazard, then builds a month-by-month expected revenue curve. Revenue starts with onboarding at month 0, then MRR begins after the free-month period. Breakeven is the first month where cumulative expected revenue is greater than or equal to CAC.
Important: churn forecasts are directional. The strongest use of this page is comparing deal structures under the same MRR and CAC assumptions, not treating the exact churn percentage as a guaranteed forecast.

Method and model assumptions

Plain-English notes for reviewers.

Churn model used
The calculator uses the controlled 12-month churn model: onboarding %, onboarding², effective free months, free months², log MRR, and default assumptions for discount/leakage controls. Onboarding has a negative linear churn effect and positive squared term, implying lower churn around ~1.1× MRR before the curve starts bending back up.
TermCoefficientInterpretation
Onboarding %-0.1158Higher onboarding is associated with lower 12-month churn initially.
Onboarding²0.0523Diminishing benefit; too much onboarding can reverse the gain.
Effective free months0.0242Small positive churn association, not a strong result.
Free months²-0.0020Slight curvature; also not strong.
log(MRR)0.0171Higher MRR slightly increases predicted churn in this model.
Revenue / CAC method
CAC is not included in the churn forecast. CAC is only used as a horizontal breakeven threshold. Expected revenue is built from cash timing: onboarding at month 0, then expected MRR after free months, multiplied by monthly survival probability and any leakage haircut.

Monthly hazard1 - (1 - churn_12mo)^(1/12)
Survival at month t(1 - monthly_hazard)^t
Month 0 cashMRR × onboarding_pct

Breakevenfirst month cumulative cash ≥ CAC
Limitations: This is a decision-support calculator based on observational data. It is best for comparing structures under the same MRR/CAC assumptions. It should not be presented as causal proof or a precise customer-level forecast.
