What this calculator is for
This page turns the effects analysis into a practical planning tool. It estimates a 12-month churn probability from the controlled churn model, converts that to a monthly churn rate, and then uses the timing of onboarding cash and recurring MRR to show when expected cumulative revenue crosses CAC.
Interpretation rule: use this to compare deal structures under the same MRR and CAC assumptions. It is most useful for “what happens if we charge 100% onboarding vs 50% onboarding?” or “what happens if we keep onboarding and give one free month?”
Executive read
For current pricing, the preferred default remains 1 free month + onboarding equal to roughly one month of MRR. The calculator makes that interpretable by showing:
• the predicted month-by-month churn path
• the expected survivors out of 100 accounts
• the revenue hit from churn each month
• the cumulative revenue build against CAC
Important caveat: CAC is not modeled as a customer behavior driver here. It is simply the dollar amount the expected cash curve must recover.

Outcome curves

These are the main decision-support visuals. Together they show churn, revenue timing, and payback.

Cumulative churn probability by month
This curve shows the cumulative chance that an average account has churned by each month. The final point at month 12 matches the 12-month churn probability estimate.
Single-account expected monthly revenue
Revenue is zero during free months, then MRR begins and gradually falls as churn reduces the survivor pool. Leakage reduces collected revenue in each paid month.
Single-account cumulative cash vs CAC
Onboarding cash lands at month 0. Cumulative expected cash rises over time as paid MRR is collected. Breakeven is the first month the blue line crosses the red CAC line.

This compares a no-churn scenario to the churn-adjusted scenario. The gap is the cumulative revenue cost of churn.

Method and assumptions

Plain-English documentation for reviewers and internal users.

Churn model used in the calculator
The calculator uses the controlled 12-month churn equation from the effects output. It includes onboarding %, onboarding², effective free months, free months², log MRR, and billing leakage. Discount-depth controls are held at zero here for simplicity.
Term Coefficient Meaning
Intercept0.2771Baseline churn level
Onboarding %-0.1158Higher onboarding reduces churn initially
Onboarding²0.0523Diminishing benefit / curvature
Free months0.0242Slightly higher churn association
Free months²-0.0020Curvature term
log(MRR)0.0171Higher MRR slightly raises predicted churn
Leakage0.5729Higher leakage aligns with worse economics
Cash and cohort math
CAC is only a threshold. The calculator builds expected revenue from timing and retention. The 100-account cohort is simply 100 times the expected per-account economics, translated into account counts and revenue.

Limitations: this is a decision-support tool built from observational regression outputs. It is best used for comparing structures and understanding timing, not as a precise causal forecast for a specific customer.
