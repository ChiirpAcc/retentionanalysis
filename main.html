Cohort & churn analysis
Raw churn rates are misleading across years — older cohorts have had more time to churn. Tenure rate normalizes this.
Why tenure rate matters
A Q1 2024 customer has had 27 months to churn. A Q1 2026 customer has had 3. Tenure rate = months paid ÷ max months since signup. A score of 0.85 means a customer paid for 85% of their possible lifetime. This metric is comparable across cohorts.

Per $100 onboarding → −0.4% of max tenure ≈ −$44 LTV. Before cohort controls this appeared to be −$92 — more than half was time bias from old $0 accounts having longer tenure. The remaining effect is small and inconsistent quarter to quarter.
